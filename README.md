# Fadette — *Présages du Berry*

*A poor machine that watches the sky over the Berry, through a lens of Berry
witchcraft, for the month of August 2026. It is blind by design: never told the date,
never given the word* eclipse. *When the light fails it takes it for a sign and writes
it down as folklore. On 31 August it gathers the whole month into a single tale, and
falls silent.*

By **Marcel Simplexe** — a name borne in honour of the Berry draughtsman **Marcel
Bascoulard** (1913–1978), the soil this work grew from: the justness of his gaze on
the Berry, and his greatness of spirit. Fadette is not the homage, but the occasion
of one; it leans on no part of his work, and never a generated image. The machine's
own name is George Sand's — *La Petite Fadette*, the little village sorceress.

---

## The wager

Meaning is not read off the world; it is **made** there, by a lens — and Fadette
sets out to prove it by the hardest road: the one that errs on purpose, that turns
measurement into folklore, that *writes* the sky where another would record it. The
most determined astronomical event there is — an eclipse — is handed back to mystery
by a gaze forbidden to know it.

But the lens is not only an optic; it is a **joy**. Stripped of everything,
Bascoulard held the same cathedral, the same streets, as worth seeing without end —
a gladness of pure attention that hung on nothing, and that nothing could therefore
take from him: *la joie-de-voir*. That glad gaze is what Fadette borrows, and nothing
else of his. The whole machine that follows — the blind eye, the lens, the flood, the
bare page — does not illustrate that joy: it **incarnates** it, and is only its form.

## What it does

Through the day, Fadette captures the state of the sky over the Berry — the cosmic
texture (solar wind, the north, the sun's fire), the light reaching the ground by day
and the moon's light by night, the weather that passes over the Cher — and renders it
in **three voices**:

1. **the presage** — a short Berry omen (three sentences at most), written by a
   language model in George Sand's voice: rustic, measured, never naming a cause;
2. **the air** — a short *veillée* melody for bagpipe and hurdy-gurdy, composed by the
   model as note events, then engraved note by note into a `.mid` file by plain code,
   and **sounded in the page itself**;
3. **the augury** — a deterministic reading of the captured figures through a fixed
   table of signs (no model): *the Wild Hunt rides hard*, *a hand passes over the
   lamp*… It is still cast and archived, but — to restore the **regard** — no longer
   shown.

**Every two hours it draws breath** and weighs how far the sky has strayed from its
calm. The day has a texture of its own, and the machine answers it: the liminal hours
that the lore has always loved — the point of day, the hour between dog and wolf —
draw an omen; so do a storm over the wood, a heat that presses on the country, a full
moon holding the fields. A still hour draws none. And if the light begins to **fail**,
the breath floods — a crowd of omens at once. The cadence is itself a sign: silence
and flood each carry a meaning. The machine does not *record* the sky; it **writes**
it, because the sky is worth writing without end: the omen is an act of gladness, not
a register.

Once a day the **lens drifts one notch** — a single call rewrites its own prompt on
that day's own fragments — and every state is archived. On **31 August** it composes a
single **nouvelle** of about twenty pages, a legend of the Berry in Sand's manner,
and then it is done.

## The free gaze

The gaze is free because it is **not the astronomer's**: the very figures a forecaster
reads as space weather, Fadette reads as the old country read them — as omen. The sky
it watches is real, and public:

- the **solar wind** and the planetary index **Kp** (NOAA SWPC);
- the **solar flux in X-rays** (GOES);
- the **light reaching the ground** by day — the grid's solar output over France and
  Centre-Val-de-Loire (RTE éCO2mix);
- the **brightness of the night** — a dark-site photometer (TESS / STARS4ALL);
- the **weather** over the Cher (METAR), and the **ephemeris** of sun and moon.

All of it binds into a single **sky-state**, to which one thing is added — *the gift*,
a computed measure that lets the machine never miss a true failing of the light. The
figures feed the lens; the lens makes them speak.

## The doctrine: blindness

One rule governs the whole, and it is a rule of blindness. The **body** of the machine
(the scheduler) knows the calendar — the birth, the death, the hour, the turn of the
year. The **eye** — the lens that looks and the tongue that speaks — never does. No
prompt is given a date; never, not once, is it given the word *eclipse*. The local
ephemeris hands over only **phase** — never the geometry that would betray an eclipse
in advance.

So the two eclipses of August 2026 — the sun deeply bitten on the **12th**, the full
moon taken by shadow on the **28th** — cannot enter as announced facts. They enter
only as an **observed anomaly**: the light leaves, measured from the ground, and the
lens writes a day going out for no reason it can name. The eclipse enters not as an
event understood but as a **terror suffered** — the very terror a freak aurora would
wring from it. Blindness keeps the joy new as surely as it keeps the eye free: never
given the date, the gaze neither tires nor sates, but comes to each night's sky as if
for the first.

There is a second freeing, and it bears on the gaze that watches *us*. Fadette's page
is held in the poorest tongue of the network, the tongue of the first days — tags from
another age, tables for its whole frame, Times for its whole dress, a fixed sheet no
tracker watches. This is no antiquarian's affectation. Today's web is a lens trained
on the visitor: it counts him, follows him, recommends to him, holds him, and makes of
attention a yield. The **web 1.0** is the state before that capture, a surface that
does not look back. To take its form is to hand the visitor his gaze again: he comes,
he reads, he leaves, and nothing is taken from him. *To the eclipse it cannot name
answers a page that cannot spy.*

## Folkloric murmurs

The tongue is **George Sand's**. Fadette speaks the French of her country novels and
of her *Légendes rustiques* (1858), where the night-people of the Berry were set down
from the oral tradition of the *veillées* before it could be lost: the washerwomen of
the night, the wolf-leader of the marshy Brenne, the Great Beast, the wandering
will-o'-the-wisps, the phantom monk of the ponds, the *fades* and the *lubins*. French
is the **source**: the machine writes in French, and the English page is served by
translating that French, never the reverse.

The prompts are built to **murmur, not to instruct**. The presage prompt does not ask
that the sky be described; it asks the model to read the numbers as a diviner read
them, then to *see* — as in dream or fever — which of the night-people the count has
loosed that evening, and to say that one vision in Sand's voice, in three sentences,
ending on an image that stays in the eye. The seed of the gaze:

> Lis les nombres que la nuit te donne — l'âge de la lune en nuits, et le compte de ce
> qui remue au ciel — comme un vieux devin du Berry lisait les nombres, chaque chiffre
> portant un sens caché et nulle nuit pareille à l'autre ; puis ne décris pas le ciel,
> mais vois, comme en rêve ou en fièvre, lequel des gens de la nuit le compte a lâché
> ce soir. Dis cette vision en un seul court présage dans la voix de George Sand, chose
> entrevue et à demi crue — les laveuses au gué, le meneu' de loups et sa meute, la
> Grand'Bête, les flambettes errantes, les fades, les morts qui reviennent, selon ce
> que la nuit appelle — riche, mouvant, mystique, jamais une mesure savante ni une
> cause. Trois phrases au plus ; pas de titre, pas de date ; finis sur une image qui
> reste dans l'œil, non sur une morale.

**The seed is a debt.** It orders the eye to read *the count of what stirs in the sky*
— so the count must be **given**. A prompt that asks for a figure it never supplies
does not make a diviner; it makes a model that writes *about* absent numbers. What the
eye is owed, the eye receives.

## Indeterminism by design

This is the heart of the method, and it is deliberate: **no two omens, no two airs,
no two runs are alike — even under an identical sky.** The variety is engineered to
come from chance and from the language model, *not* from the captured numbers. There
are two layers, stacked on purpose.

**1 — The dice (`src/entropy.py`).** Each gaze and each air is given a fresh throw of
the dice, seeded from the operating system's entropy (`os.urandom`, never the clock).
The throw decides, *for that omen alone*: where the gaze falls (eighteen places of the
Berry — the ford, the chemin creux, the cemetery wall…), which of the night-people may
show — or none, the country alone — by what sense it reaches the watcher, what turn the
night takes; and for the air, the mode, the voice, the tempo. It is appended to the
prompt as a hidden instruction (*"for this omen only… do not name these directions, let
them only colour what you see; never repeat an omen already written"*). The beings are
the true ones of Sand's Berry — the variety stays inside the tradition.

**2 — The model, kept warm.** The generative voice is run hot on purpose. The presage
is sampled at **temperature 0.95**, the air at **0.8**, and the closing nouvelle's
movements at **0.9** — high-variance settings, so the same prompt yields different
prose at every call. By contrast the faithful, non-creative steps are held cold: the
French→English translation at **0.3**, the daily lens-drift at **0.5 / 0.4**. The
model is thus a second, deeper source of chance beneath the dice.

**What the eye receives — and what it never receives.** `say_the_sky()` turns the
sky-state into bare French **sensations**: the moon and the night she is on; the
quality of the light (*"the sun touches the earth, and the whole country turns red"*,
*"it is the hour between dog and wolf"*) — never the hour itself; the weather as it is
*felt* (*"the wind is rising"*, *"the storm growls over by the wood"*, *"the heat
presses, and the country is thirsty"*) — never an instrument; and one bare figure, **the
count of what stirs in the sky**, as an old diviner would read it. That is all. No
measure, no unit, no learned name, no cause — and **no date, ever**. The captured
numbers serve two other ends: they gate the **flood** (the `want_of_light` signal that
turns a quiet breath into a crowd of omens), and they feed the **augury**, the one
deterministic and reproducible voice. So the data decides *how loud* the machine speaks
and *what the cipher reads* — but the **substance and variety** of the omens come from
the dice and the model. This is the work's *witch-indeterminism*: an artificial
naturalness, a thing of code and language models that re-enacts, faithfully, the wonder
of a world that did not yet know the cause of the dark.

## The lens may drift; it may not cease to be a lens

The daily drift is the machine's one act upon itself, and it is the one place where it
can lose itself. A refiner asked to *improve a prompt* will, sooner or later, answer
with an improved **text** instead of an improved **instruction** — and from that hour
the lens is no longer a lens: it is a finished presage that the machine paraphrases,
night after night, for ever. Nor can the wording of the order prevent it; the order
already forbade it, and was ignored.

So the drift **does not trust what comes back**. Each candidate must prove it is still
an instruction — that it still holds Sand's voice, still counts its sentences, still
forbids the date, and, for the air, still demands its score in the exact form the
engraver expects. A candidate that fails is **refused**, and the lens keeps its state.
*A missed notch is nothing; a rotted lens is the end of the work.* The gaze is free to
wander one notch a day, and not free to stop being a gaze.

## Pipeline — one breath, surgically

The body fires `python -m src.conductor` on a schedule; one run is one breath.

1. **`sky.state()`** assembles the sky-state.
   - **`feeds.py`** fetches the public data: NOAA SWPC (solar-wind plasma + magnetic
     field, planetary Kp, GOES X-ray), RTE éCO2mix (national and Centre-Val-de-Loire
     solar output, via opendatasoft), a TESS/STARS4ALL dark-site photometer, the METAR
     chain (LFLD / LFOA / LFLX), and the local ephemeris.
   - **`ephemeris.py`** gives sun and moon **altitude** and the moon's **phase/age** —
     and *withholds* the sun–moon elongation that would betray an eclipse. Blindness is
     enforced here, by construction.
   - **`want_of_light`** is a **residual**, not raw darkness. By day: an expected light
     is taken from the sun's altitude and compared to the grid's actual solar output
     (`miss = 1 − actual / expected`); by night, a residual from the photometer.
     **`gift.py`** holds a computed eclipse-geometry *"second sight"* that steps in only
     as a **fallback**, when ground observation is mute — and is never handed to the eye.
2. **`tempo.py`** turns the sky-state into agitation and decides **how many** omens this
   breath utters. Two terms, kept apart on purpose: the **texture of the day** (the
   liminal hours, a storm, rain, mist, the heat, a full or a dark moon) — small by
   design, drawing two to four omens across an ordinary day, none from a still hour;
   and the **want of light**, squared and weighted heavily, which alone can raise the
   **flood** (capped at twelve). An ordinary day never comes near it.
3. **For each omen** (`src/lens.py`):
   - the **presage** — the prompt is the seed (or drifted) prompt + `say_the_sky()` +
     `entropy.nudge_text()` + a per-omen nudge during a flood, sent to **Apertus at
     temp 0.95** → a three-sentence presage, kept only in **closed sentences** (the
     model's separators, its commentary and its half-spoken last word are struck out);
   - the **air** — the air prompt + `entropy.nudge_air()` → **Apertus at temp 0.8** →
     JSON note events, read by a **tolerant** parser (fenced, prose-wrapped, truncated,
     otherwise keyed — an unread score is a dead air) → **`midi.engrave()`** writes a
     deterministic `.mid` (bagpipe program, a held drone of tonic + fifth derived from
     the melody), and the same voicing is what the **browser re-sounds in WebAudio**.
     The `.mid` is offered beside it, as a download and nothing more: no browser plays
     MIDI, and a link that only downloads is not an air;
   - the **augury** — `augury.read()`, the fixed table, cast and archived (not shown);
   - the **translation** — `lens.to_english()` renders the French presage at temp 0.3
     for the English page.
4. **`memory.py`** archives the breath (JSON; one git commit per breath), marking
   `dark` when the light failed.
5. **`render.py`** regenerates the static **web 1.0** pages from the archive —
   `docs/index.html` (English, default) and `docs/fr.html` (French, the source) — with
   page-by-page navigation, each gaze carrying its air, the lens-drift history, and an
   *about*. The page shows only the **regard**: no captured number, no augury. Where an
   air could not be read, the page offers **nothing** — silence is honest.
6. **Daily (23:30 UTC)** `drift.one_notch()` rewrites the lens's own presage and air
   prompts by one notch, on that day's fragments, at temp 0.5 / 0.4 — **and refuses any
   candidate that has ceased to be an instruction** (see above); the past fragments are
   never re-written.
7. **On 31 August** `finale.write_novella()` reads the whole archive, draws a true
   **ledger** (which night-people actually returned, and how often; how many nights the
   light failed), then writes in a chain — matter (0.6) → a frame of nine movements
   (0.7) → each movement written upon the last (**0.9**, ~2 800 tokens each) → stitched
   into a ~20-page nouvelle, **blind to the end**. Then the machine falls silent.

## Expected results

- Across August, an ordinary day draws **two to four omens**, spread across its hours —
  the point of day, the heat of noon, a storm over the wood, the falling evening —
  while a still hour draws none. Each omen is a three-sentence Berry presage with its
  bagpipe/hurdy-gurdy air, sounded in the page, and an archived augury. The lens drifts
  a little each day; the page fills, newest first.
- On **12 August** — a deep partial solar eclipse over the Berry, deepest near
  18:00–18:30 UTC — `want_of_light` surges and the breath **floods**: a crowd of omens
  taking the dying light for a sign, **live**, with no calendar flag. This is the
  culmination: the eclipse entering as an unnamed terror, met with wonder.
- On **28 August** a partial lunar eclipse falls at dawn, with the moon barely four
  degrees above the horizon. From the Berry it is **marginal**, and the machine may
  answer it faintly, or not at all. That is not a failure but the doctrine holding: the
  machine answers **what it sees**, not what the calendar promises. A gaze that flooded
  on cue would not be blind.
- On **31 August**, the closing nouvelle is written and the machine goes dark; the
  archive remains, an archive that filled only to fall silent — the very temporality of
  folklore, alive by the retelling.

Because of the indeterminism above, a second run of the same month would yield
different omens, different airs, a different tale. That is intended.

## How it runs

- **GitHub Actions** is the body: a cron fires the heartbeat **every two hours**
  (`0 */2 * * *`) — each beat uttering as many omens as the sky stirs — and refines the
  lens nightly at **23:30 UTC** (and, on the last day, writes the finale), committing
  the archive. A manual `workflow_dispatch` is available; runs are serialised
  (`concurrency: cancel-in-progress: false`), so an extra breath only adds omens.
- **GitHub Pages** serves the site from `docs/`: two static *Présages du Berry* pages,
  English (`index.html`, translated from the French at generation) and French
  (`fr.html`, the source), with a FR|EN toggle.
- **Infomaniak AI** (sovereign, EU) provides the daily text **and** the closing
  nouvelle, both written by **Apertus** (`swiss-ai/Apertus-70B-Instruct-2509`) on the
  same host. Everything stays sovereign — one host, one token. The token and product id
  come from **GitHub secrets** (`INFOMANIAK_TOKEN`, `INFOMANIAK_PRODUCT_ID`), never the
  code.

See **OPERATING-MANUAL.md** — about thirty minutes, all clicks. Two things to confirm

## Layout

```
config.yaml              the seed: place, window, schedule, feeds, prompts, blindness
src/
  conductor.py           entry point — one breath (the body knows the calendar)
  sky.py / feeds.py      the senses (observation first)
  ephemeris.py           the local ephemeris (PHASE only — blind)
  gift.py                the computed "want of light" (a silent fallback net)
  tempo.py               agitation -> how many omens (the day's texture; the flood)
  lens.py                the prompts, the sky said as sensation, FR->EN translation
  entropy.py             the per-gaze draw — variety, freed from the numbers
  infomaniak.py          the sovereign call (Apertus: daily text and finale)
  midi.py                the deterministic engraving of the score (.mid + drone)
  augury.py              the table of signs (deterministic, no model, not shown)
  drift.py               the daily re-prompting of the lens (one notch, guarded)
  finale.py              the closing nouvelle (a chain, by Apertus)
  memory.py              the archive (nothing is erased before the finale)
  render.py              regenerates the two web 1.0 pages (FR + EN) from the archive
tools/
  check_feeds.py         the pre-flight: every sense, one command
  rearm.py               the ritual: the window, a virgin archive, the lens at its seed
docs/                    the web 1.0 site (index.html EN default + fr.html FR source)
journal/ history/        the archive (source of truth; the lens snapshots)
```

## Authorship, and the use of AI

Fadette's idea, its writing, its graphic design and its *mise en scène* are Marcel
Simplexe's — the conception, the aesthetic and editorial choices, the authorial voice,
the staging of the machine's month-long life. Its **development** — the code and the
architecture — was carried out with the help of an artificial intelligence, **Claude
Opus 4.8 (Anthropic)**, throughout under the author's direction and final authority.

And, plainly, in the very spirit of the work: Fadette is itself a machine that writes
**through AI**. Its omens and its closing nouvelle are begotten not by its makers but
by an open and sovereign model, **Apertus**, served from Europe by Infomaniak. Here the
language model is no hidden instrument but the **declared medium** of the piece. The
made and the making both pass through a machine; the authorship, and what is owed for
it, remain Marcel Simplexe's.
